01
Pointing at Things, Places & Groups
这个 · 这里/这儿 · 这些 · 这边, and their 那 / 哪 cousins
个 · The Measure Word

这个 = 这 + 个.

Chinese inserts a measure word between a pointer (or number) and a noun. 个 (gè) is the default, all-purpose one. You will use it constantly.

Pointer + 个 + Noun
这个人是我朋友。
Zhège rén shì wǒ péngyou.
This person is my friend.
Standalone "that one"
我要那个。
Wǒ yào nàge.
I want that one.

There are many measure words (本 for books, 杯 for cups…) but 个 is the safe default. When in doubt: 个.

Place Suffixes

-里 and -儿: same meaning, different mouth.

Both suffixes turn a pointer into a place. They are basically interchangeable. The split is regional and register-based.

这里
zhèlǐ
here
那里
nàlǐ
there
哪里
nǎlǐ
where?
这儿
zhèr
here
那儿
nàr
there
哪儿
nǎr
where?

-里 is more standard and slightly more written; you'll hear it everywhere, especially in the south. -儿 is the colloquial, Beijing/northern flavor. Pick one and stay consistent within a sentence.

Scope & Plurality

These, those, some.

这些
zhèxiē · these
Definite group near you.
这些都是新书。
那些
nàxiē · those
Definite group over there.
那些是我的衣服。
有的
yǒude · some (of them)
Picks a subset out of a known set.
有的人喜欢茶。
有些
yǒuxiē · some / a few
Closer to "a few", more open.
有些菜很好吃。

这些 / 那些 = specific "these / those" (you can point at them). 有的 / 有些 = unspecified "some" (you can't necessarily point). Don't sweat the 有的 / 有些 split early, they overlap a lot.

Direction

This way, that way.

边 (biān) literally means "side". Stuck on a pointer, it becomes a direction.

这边
zhèbiān · this side / this way
请到这边来。
Please come this way.
那边
nàbiān · that side / over there
他在那边。
He's over that way.

The same trick works with 哪 to ask 哪边？ "Which way?" (not in today's 25 but obvious once you see the pattern).

02
The Question-Word Family
什么 · 谁 · 哪 · 几 · 多少 · 怎么 · 怎么样
The One Rule

Question words don't move.

English drags question words to the front: "Who is he?" Chinese does the opposite. The question word sits exactly where the answer would go. Same word order as a statement.

Statement
他是 Leo。
Tā shì Leo.
He is Leo.
Swap the name for 谁
他是 谁？
Tā shì shéi?
Who is he?
Statement
你去 学校。
Nǐ qù xuéxiào.
You're going to school.
Swap the place for 哪里
你去 哪里？
Nǐ qù nǎlǐ?
Where are you going?

No 吗 on wh-questions. The question word already does the marking. 他是谁吗？ is wrong. Just 他是谁？.

How Many?

几 vs 多少.

Both mean "how many / much", but they're not interchangeable.

几
jǐ · small countable
Used when you expect a small number (usually under 10). Always needs a measure word.
你家有几口人？
How many people in your family?
多少
duōshao · open / large
Used for larger or unknown quantities, and for money. Measure word optional.
这个多少钱？
How much is this?

Rule of thumb: if you'd be surprised by the answer being above 10, use 几. Otherwise 多少. Money is always 多少钱.

How?

怎么 vs 怎么样.

Two flavors of "how". Different grammar slots, easy to keep apart once you see them side by side.

怎么 + Verb · "in what way…"
这个字怎么读？
Zhège zì zěnme dú?
How do you read this character?
怎么样 · standalone "how is it?"
今天天气怎么样？
Jīntiān tiānqì zěnmeyàng?
How's the weather today?
怎么样 · as a casual "what do you think?"
这个怎么样？
Zhège zěnmeyàng?
How's this one? / What do you think?

怎么 needs a verb. 怎么样 can stand alone as the whole question.

03
First Numbers
零 · 一 · 二, plus a small surprise about 一
0 · 1 · 2

Three numbers to start.

零
líng · zero
零下三度。
Three below zero.
一
yī · one
我有一个哥哥。
I have one older brother.
二
èr · two
二月很冷。
February is very cold.

一 changes tone depending on what follows: yī alone or counting, yí before a 4th tone (一个 yí gè), yì before everything else (一天 yì tiān). This is your first taste of tone sandhi. Don't drill it yet, just notice when it happens.

二 vs 两: 二 is the number "two" (counting, dates, phone numbers). For "two of something" you'll use 两 (liǎng), 两个人, not 二个人. We meet 两 in a later lesson.

Dialogue 1 · Full

Asking about people

A
你好！请问，他是谁？
Nǐ hǎo! Qǐngwèn, tā shì shéi?
Hi! Excuse me, who is he?
B
他是我的朋友 Leo。
Tā shì wǒ de péngyou Leo.
He's my friend Leo.
A
他是哪国人？
Tā shì nǎ guó rén?
What nationality is he?
B
他是美国人。你呢？
Tā shì Měiguó rén. Nǐ ne?
He's American. And you?
A
我是加拿大人。
Wǒ shì Jiānádà rén.
I'm Canadian.
B
你叫什么名字？
Nǐ jiào shénme míngzi?
What's your name?
A
我叫 Maya。
Wǒ jiào Maya.
I'm Maya.
B
很高兴认识你！
Hěn gāoxìng rènshi nǐ!
Nice to meet you!

Notice: 谁 / 哪 / 什么 all sit where the answer goes. No 吗 anywhere, these are wh-questions.

Dialogue 2 · Full

This one or that one?

A
这是什么？
Zhè shì shénme?
What's this?
B
这是茶。
Zhè shì chá.
This is tea.
A
这个多少钱？
Zhège duōshao qián?
How much is this one?
B
这个二十。那个怎么样？
Zhège èrshí. Nàge zěnmeyàng?
This one's twenty. How about that one?
A
那个也好。这些呢？
Nàge yě hǎo. Zhèxiē ne?
That one's good too. And these?
B
这些都是新的。
Zhèxiē dōu shì xīn de.
These are all new.
A
我要这个。
Wǒ yào zhège.
I'll take this one.
B
好的，谢谢！
Hǎo de, xièxie!
Sure, thank you!

Six new pointers in eight lines: 什么 · 这 · 这个 · 那个 · 这些 · 多少 · 怎么样. This is exactly the rhythm of pointing-and-asking in a shop.
