Pinyin & Tones
Learning to read and say Chinese sounds correctly
Pinyin 拼音

Your pronunciation map.

Pinyin is the standard system for writing Mandarin pronunciation using the Latin alphabet. It helps you say words before you can fully read characters. Every syllable has three parts.

hǎo
Initial
h
Final
ao
Tone
3rd ˇ

Pinyin is not English spelling in disguise. x, q, zh, c, z are all different from their English cousins.

The Tones

4 tones + 1 neutral.

Each syllable carries a tone. Change the tone, change the word.

mā

The Ma Family

One syllable, five words.

The full tone family for ma shows exactly how tones work and introduces 吗, the most useful beginner grammar particle.

吗 has no tone mark: it is a neutral-tone particle. It turns any statement into a yes/no question. You will use it constantly starting in Lesson 1.

c / z

02
Radicals & Characters
How Chinese characters are built and why it matters
Radicals 部首

Characters have structure.

Chinese characters are not random drawings. They are built from recurring components called radicals. Radicals often hint at meaning or category.

亻
Person
Appears in 你, 他, 们: all person-related characters
口
Mouth
In 吗, 呢, 叫, 吃: speech, sound, mouth
女
Woman
In 她, 妈: female-related characters

Good rule: radicals often give useful clues. Not: "the radical tells me the exact meaning." That is the sane version.

Spot the Radical

Find the clue inside.

你
nǐ: you
Left side: 亻 person radical. Right side hints at the sound.
她
tā: she
Left side: 女 woman radical. Tells you: female person.
吗
ma: question particle
Left side: 口 mouth radical. It is a spoken particle: makes sense.
国
guó: country
Enclosure structure: 囗 surrounds 玉. A territory enclosing something valuable.
03
Your First Words
Greetings and courtesy: the phrases you will use every day
Greetings 问候

Eight phrases. Infinite utility.

These are not optional extras: they are the entry point to every Chinese conversation you will ever have.

CharacterPinyinEnglish
你好Nǐ hǎoHello
你好吗？Nǐ hǎo ma?How are you?
我很好。Wǒ hěn hǎo.I am well.
谢谢XièxieThank you
不客气Bú kèqiYou're welcome
对不起DuìbuqǐSorry / Excuse me
没关系Méi guānxiIt's okay / No problem
再见ZàijiànGoodbye

你好 is not casual: it is the standard neutral greeting. For casual friends: 嗨 (hāi) or 哈喽 (hāluó). For formal situations, 你好 is always safe.

Inside the Phrases

Nothing is arbitrary.

Every greeting is built from characters you will see again and again.

你好
你 (nǐ) = you  +  好 (hǎo) = good
Literally: "You good." Tone-perfect: nǐ hǎo.
谢谢
Same character doubled for emphasis. 谢 (xiè) = thank. Saying it twice is polite and natural.
对不起
对 = correct  ·  不起 = cannot stand up to. "I cannot live up to your expectations."
再见
再 (zài) = again  +  见 (jiàn) = see. "See you again." Same logic as au revoir.
