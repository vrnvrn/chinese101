Saying What Is, What You Want, What You Like
是, 有, 在, the wanting verbs, the three "can" verbs, and your first adjectives
A is B

是 links two nouns.

是 (shì) connects one noun to another: A is B. Use it for names, jobs, and what something is.

我是学生。
Wǒ shì xuésheng.
I am a student.
他是医生。
Tā shì yīsheng.
He is a doctor.
这是我的书。
Zhè shì wǒ de shū.
This is my book.

是 joins two nouns. Do not put 是 before an adjective: for that you use 很, on a later slide.

Have and Not Have

有, and its only negative 没有.

我有一个朋友。
Wǒ yǒu yí gè péngyou.
I have a friend.
我没有钱。
Wǒ méiyǒu qián.
I have no money.
你有时间吗？
Nǐ yǒu shíjiān ma?
Do you have time?

有 means "have". Its negative is always 没有, never 不有. 没有 also means "did not" for past actions.

Where Things Are

在 plus a place.

我在家。
Wǒ zài jiā.
I am at home.
老师在学校。
Lǎoshī zài xuéxiào.
The teacher is at school.
你在哪儿？
Nǐ zài nǎr?
Where are you?

在 plus a place says where someone or something is. You already met 在 inside 现在 (now), which is "at this time".

Names and Knowing

叫, 问, 认识, 知道.

你叫什么名字？
Nǐ jiào shénme míngzi?
What is your name?
我认识他。
Wǒ rènshi tā.
I know him.
我知道。
Wǒ zhīdào.
I know.

认识 is to know or recognize a person; 知道 is to know a fact. 叫 is "to be called", and 问 is "to ask".

Thinking and Wanting

觉得, 想, 要.

觉得 = think, feel
我觉得很好。
Wǒ juéde hěn hǎo.
I think it is good.
想 + verb = would like to
我想喝茶。
Wǒ xiǎng hē chá.
I would like to drink tea.
要 + noun = want
我要茶。
Wǒ yào chá.
I want tea.

想 plus a verb is "would like to do". 要 plus a noun is "want a thing". The negative 不要 means "do not want" or "do not".

Love and Like

喜欢 and 爱.

我喜欢中文。
Wǒ xǐhuan Zhōngwén.
I like Chinese.
我喜欢看书。
Wǒ xǐhuan kàn shū.
I like reading.
我爱我的家人。
Wǒ ài wǒ de jiārén.
I love my family.

喜欢 is "to like", and it can take a noun or a verb. 爱 is "to love", stronger, for the people you are close to.

The Three "Can" Verbs

会, 能, 可以.

All three can translate as "can", but each has its own job.

会 = a learned skill
我会说中文。
Wǒ huì shuō Zhōngwén.
I can speak Chinese.
能 = able to, circumstances allow
我今天能来。
Wǒ jīntiān néng lái.
I can come today.
可以 = allowed to, permission
我可以走吗？
Wǒ kěyǐ zǒu ma?
May I go?

会 is for skills you have learned, 能 is for being able to under the circumstances, and 可以 is for permission.

Describing

Adjectives take 很, not 是.

To describe something, link the subject to the adjective with 很. There is no 是 before an adjective.

Subject + 很 + adjective
这个很大。
Zhège hěn dà.
This one is big.
东西很贵。
Dōngxi hěn guì.
Things are expensive.
我很好。
Wǒ hěn hǎo.
I am well.

Today's adjectives: 大 big, 小 small, 多 many, 少 few, 好 good, 新 new, 贵 expensive. Say 我很好, never 我是好. Here 很 is just a link, not a strong "very".

Putting It Together

A few lines about yourself.

我是学生，我有很多同学。
Wǒ shì xuésheng, wǒ yǒu hěn duō tóngxué.
I am a student, and I have many classmates.
我喜欢中文，我觉得中文很好。
Wǒ xǐhuan Zhōngwén, wǒ juéde Zhōngwén hěn hǎo.
I like Chinese, and I think Chinese is great.
我会说一点儿中文。
Wǒ huì shuō yìdiǎnr Zhōngwén.
I can speak a little Chinese.
我想买新书，新书很贵。
Wǒ xiǎng mǎi xīn shū, xīn shū hěn guì.
I want to buy new books, but new books are expensive.
