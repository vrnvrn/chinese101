02
想: To Want
Expressing desire and intention
Grammar: 想

想 + verb = "want to ___"

想 (xiǎng) is a modal verb meaning "want to" or "would like to." Place it directly before the action verb. It can also mean "to think / to miss someone."

想
xiǎng
want to / would like to
不想
bù xiǎng
don't want to
想吃
xiǎng chī
want to eat
I want to eat rice
我想吃米饭。
Wǒ xiǎng chī mǐfàn.
I want to eat rice.
What do you want to drink?
你想喝什么？
Nǐ xiǎng hē shénme?
What do you want to drink?
I'd like tea, please
我想喝茶，谢谢。
Wǒ xiǎng hē chá, xièxie.
I'd like tea, thank you.

想 is also used for "to think" (我想他很好 = I think he's great) and "to miss someone" (我想你 = I miss you). Context makes it clear.

03
Money & Prices 钱
多少钱？: How much?
钱 Money

How much does it cost?

元/块
yuán / kuài
The main unit. 块 is the spoken form (like "dollar").
角/毛
jiǎo / máo
One tenth of a yuan. 毛 is the spoken form (like "dime").
分
fēn
One cent (1/100 yuan). Rarely used in daily speech.
¥ 35.50
三十五块五毛
sānshíwǔ kuài wǔ máo
How much is this?
这个多少钱？
Zhège duōshao qián?
How much does this cost?
It costs twenty yuan
二十块钱。
Èrshí kuài qián.
Twenty yuan.

多少 (duōshao) = how many / how much (for large or unknown quantities). For small quantities you already know, use 几 (jǐ) (from Lesson 3).

Key Pattern

太___了: "So ___! / Too ___!"

Sandwich any adjective between 太 (tài) and 了 (le) to intensify it. This is one of the most expressive patterns in everyday Chinese.

So delicious!
太好吃了！
Tài hǎochī le!
So delicious! / This is amazing!
Too expensive!
太贵了！
Tài guì le!
Too expensive! (used when bargaining)
So happy!
太高兴了！
Tài gāoxìng le!
So happy! / I'm so glad!
A little cheaper, please
便宜一点儿，好吗？
Piányí yīdiǎnr, hǎo ma?
Can you make it a little cheaper? (bargaining)

一点儿 (yīdiǎnr) = a little. Add it after an adjective: 便宜一点儿 = "a little cheaper." Or after a verb: 吃一点儿 = "eat a little."

More Useful Words

都 · 一些 · 一点儿

都
dōu
all / both. Covers everything in the subject.
一些
yīxiē
some / a few. Used before nouns.
一点儿
yīdiǎnr
a little. Used after verbs or adjectives.
We all like Chinese food
我们都喜欢中国菜。
Wǒmen dōu xǐhuān Zhōngguó cài.
We all like Chinese food.
I'd like some fruit
我想要一些水果。
Wǒ xiǎng yào yīxiē shuǐguǒ.
I'd like some fruit.

都 always comes after the subject and before the verb. "我们都喜欢" not "都我们喜欢." Place it right before the verb, always.

Dialogue 1 · Full

Ordering at a restaurant.

A
你好！请问，你们想吃什么？
Nǐ hǎo! Qǐngwèn, nǐmen xiǎng chī shénme?
Hello! Excuse me, what would you like to eat?
B
我想吃米饭和菜。她想吃面条。
Wǒ xiǎng chī mǐfàn hé cài. Tā xiǎng chī miàntiáo.
I want rice and a dish. She wants noodles.
A
好的！你们想喝什么？
Hǎo de! Nǐmen xiǎng hē shénme?
Sure! What would you like to drink?
B
我们都想喝茶，谢谢。
Wǒmen dōu xiǎng hē chá, xièxie.
We both want tea, thank you.
A
这是你们的茶。菜好吃吗？
Zhè shì nǐmen de chá. Cài hǎochī ma?
Here's your tea. Is the food delicious?
B
太好吃了！谢谢！
Tài hǎochī le! Xièxie!
So delicious! Thank you!
A
请问，多少钱？
Qǐngwèn, duōshao qián?
Excuse me, how much is it?
B
一共八十八块。
Yīgòng bāshíbā kuài.
Eighty-eight yuan in total.

请问 (qǐngwèn) = excuse me / may I ask · 一共 (yīgòng) = in total / altogether · 这是 (zhè shì) = this is / here is

Shopping for clothes.

Dialogue 2 · Full

Shopping for clothes.

A
你好！我想买衣服。
Nǐ hǎo! Wǒ xiǎng mǎi yīfu.
Hello! I want to buy clothes.
B
好的！这件衣服很漂亮，怎么样？
Hǎo de! Zhè jiàn yīfu hěn piàoliang, zěnme yàng?
Sure! This piece of clothing is very pretty, what do you think?
A
很漂亮！多少钱？
Hěn piàoliang! Duōshao qián?
Very pretty! How much is it?
B
二百八十块。
Èrbǎi bāshí kuài.
Two hundred and eighty yuan.
A
太贵了！便宜一点儿，好吗？
Tài guì le! Piányí yīdiǎnr, hǎo ma?
Too expensive! Can you make it a little cheaper?
B
好吧，两百四十块。这已经很便宜了。
Hǎo ba, liǎngbǎi sìshí kuài. Zhè yǐjīng hěn piányí le.
Okay, two hundred and forty yuan. This is already very cheap.
A
好！我买这件。谢谢！
Hǎo! Wǒ mǎi zhè jiàn. Xièxie!
Okay! I'll buy this one. Thank you!

件 (jiàn) = measure word for clothing · 好吧 (hǎo ba) = "okay then / fine" · 已经 (yǐjīng) = already · 买 (mǎi) = to buy
