Meeting Someone New

A first meeting covering greetings, names, age, nationality, jobs, and hobbies. Practice line by line or read the full script below.

A Line 1 / 16
你好！
Nǐ hǎo!
Hello!
Full Script
01
Greetings
你好 · 你好吗？ · 再见
Key Phrases
Saying hello and goodbye.
CharacterPinyinEnglish
你好Nǐ hǎoHello
你好吗？Nǐ hǎo ma?How are you?
我很好。Wǒ hěn hǎo.I am well.
你呢？Nǐ ne?And you?
不客气。Bú kèqi.You are welcome.
再见！Zàijiàn!Goodbye!
Grammar
Patterns to know.
Statement
我很好。
Wǒ hěn hǎo.
I am well. (literally: I very good.)
Yes/No Question
你好吗？
Nǐ hǎo ma?
How are you? (literally: You good [question]?)
Follow-up Question
你呢？
Nǐ ne?
And you? (a short way to redirect a question back)

Adding 吗 (ma) to the end of any statement turns it into a yes/no question. No word order change is needed. 你好 means Hello. 你好吗 means How are you?

02
Introductions
你叫什么名字？ · 我叫___
Key Phrases
Sharing your name.
CharacterPinyinEnglish
叫jiàoto be called / to call
名字míngziname
什么shénmewhat
我叫___。Wǒ jiào ___.My name is ___.
你叫什么名字？Nǐ jiào shénme míngzi?What is your name?
很高兴认识你。Hěn gāoxìng rènshi nǐ.Nice to meet you.
Grammar
Name patterns.
Stating your name
我叫 Maya。
Wǒ jiào Maya.
My name is Maya. (literally: I called Maya.)
Asking someone's name
你叫什么名字？
Nǐ jiào shénme míngzi?
What is your name? (literally: You called what name?)
Nice to meet you
很高兴认识你。
Hěn gāoxìng rènshi nǐ.
Nice to meet you. (literally: Very happy to know you.)

Question words like 什么 (what) stay in the same position as the answer. In 你叫什么名字, the word 什么 sits exactly where the name would go. No word order flip is needed.

Use 你多大 for adults and 你几岁 for small children. Numbers from 11 to 99 follow a logical pattern: 十 (ten) + digit for teens, and digit + 十 + digit for larger numbers. 二十五 is literally "two ten five."

Grammar
Nationality pattern.
Asking nationality
你是哪国人？
Nǐ shì nǎ guó rén?
What nationality are you?
Stating nationality
我是英国人。
Wǒ shì Yīngguó rén.
I am British.

Adding 人 (person) after a country name gives the nationality. 美国 (America) plus 人 equals 美国人 (American). 中国 (China) plus 人 equals 中国人 (Chinese person). This pattern works for almost every country.

Grammar
Job patterns.
Asking about someone's job
你做什么工作？

What work do you do?
Stating your job
我是程序员。
Wǒ shì chéngyùyuán.
I am a programmer.

Use 我是 followed by the occupation to state your job. This is the same 是 used for nationality. Chinese does not use articles like "a" or "the," so 我是学生 covers both "I am a student" and "I am the student."

Grammar
Hobby patterns.
Asking about hobbies
你有什么爱好？
Nǐ yǒu shénme àihào?
What hobbies do you have?
Saying what you like
我喜欢跑步和看电影。
Wǒ xǐhuān pǎobù hé kàn diànyǐng.
I like running and watching movies.
Stating your hobby
我的爱好是读书。
Wǒ de àihào shì dúshū.
My hobby is reading.

和 (hé) means "and" when listing items. 我喜欢跑步和游泳 means "I like running and swimming." You can list as many hobbies as you like by joining them with 和. Note that 和 connects nouns and noun phrases, not verbs.
