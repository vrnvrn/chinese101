Pinyin & Tones
Learning to read and say Chinese sounds correctly
Pinyin 拼音

Your pronunciation map.

Pinyin is the standard system for writing Mandarin pronunciation using the Latin alphabet. It helps you say words before you can fully read characters. Every syllable has three parts.

hǎo
Initial
h
Final
ao
Tone
3rd ˇ

Pinyin is not English spelling in disguise. x, q, zh, c, z are all different from their English cousins.

The Tones

4 tones + 1 neutral.

Each syllable carries a tone. Change the tone, change the word.

mā

The Ma Family

One syllable, five words.

The full tone family for ma shows exactly how tones work and introduces 吗, the most useful beginner grammar particle.

吗 has no tone mark: it is a neutral-tone particle. It turns any statement into a yes/no question. You will use it constantly.

Pinyin Sounds

Watch out for these.

English instincts will occasionally try to sabotage the mission.

c / z

02
Radicals & Characters
How Chinese characters are built and why it matters
Radicals 部首

Characters have structure.

Chinese characters are not random drawings. They are built from recurring components called radicals. Radicals often hint at meaning or category.

亻
Person
Appears in 你, 他, 们: all person-related characters
口
Mouth
In 吗, 呢, 叫, 吃: speech, sound, mouth
女
Woman
In 她, 妈: female-related characters

Good rule: radicals often give useful clues. Not: "the radical tells me the exact meaning." That is the sane version.

Spot the Radical

Find the clue inside.

你
nǐ: you
Left side: 亻 person radical. Right side hints at the sound.
她
tā: she
Left side: 女 woman radical. Tells you: female person.
吗
ma: question particle
Left side: 口 mouth radical. It is a spoken particle: makes sense.
他
tā: he
Left side: 亻 person radical. Right side 也 hints at the sound.

Words 12–22 · Pronouns

I, you, he, she, it. And their plurals.

Chinese pronouns follow a simple pattern: singular + 们 (men) = plural. Same character, no conjugation.

#CharacterPinyinEnglish
12我wǒI, me
13你nǐyou (singular)
14他tāhe, him
15她tāshe, her
16它tāit
17我们wǒmenwe, us
18你们nǐmenyou (plural)
19他们tāmenthey (m./mixed)
20她们tāmenthey (all female)
21它们tāmenthey (things / animals)
22大家dàjiāeveryone

他 / 她 / 它 are all pronounced tā. In speech, gender is invisible. In writing, the radical (亻 / 女) tells you the gender. 大家好！ = "Hello, everyone!", the standard opener for any audience.

Words 23–25 · Demonstratives

This and that.

Three more words round out the first 25: the first two of the demonstrative family.

这是我的书。 Zhè shì wǒ de shū. = "This is my book."

Pair with a measure word (个 ge) to point at one specific thing: 这个 = this one.

04
Grammar
How Chinese builds sentences, and why it is simpler than you expect
Yes/No Questions

Statement + 吗 = question.

English flips word order to make a question. Mandarin adds 吗 at the end. That is it.

Statement
你是美国人。
Nǐ shì Měiguó rén.
You are American.
Question: add 吗
你是美国人吗？
Nǐ shì Měiguó rén ma?
Are you American?

Chinese does not rely on rising intonation alone. Add 吗. Much cleaner than English inversion.

Core Sentence Patterns

Four patterns. All of Lesson 1.

Subject + Verb + Object
我叫 Maya。
Wǒ jiào Maya.
My name is Maya. (lit. "I am called Maya")
Subject + 是 + Noun
我是美国人。
Wǒ shì Měiguó rén.
I am American.
Subject + 很 + Adjective
我很好。
Wǒ hěn hǎo.
I am well. (很 acts as a linker, not just "very")
Statement + 吗 = Yes/No Question
你是学生吗？
Nǐ shì xuéshēng ma?
Are you a student?
Grammar Notes

What Chinese drops.

Mandarin is often simpler than English. Here is what it does not bother with:

No articles
No "a" or "the".
我是学生 = I am a student. Just like that.
Verbs don't conjugate
我是 · 你是 · 她是. The same 是 for everyone. No am/are/is changes.
Plurals are often context
Often no plural marking needed. Context carries it.
Tense by context
Verbs don't change for past/present/future. Time words handle it. For Day 1, stay present.
