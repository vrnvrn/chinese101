02
在: Location
The most important preposition in Chinese
Grammar: 在

在 does three things.

在 (zài) is a preposition meaning "at / in / on." It can appear before a place (as a verb) or before a verb to show ongoing action.

As a verb
我在家。
I am at home.
As a preposition
我在学校学习。
I study at school.
With 住
我住在北京。
I live in Beijing.
Where are you?
你在哪儿？/ 你在哪里？
Nǐ zài nǎr? / Nǐ zài nǎlǐ?
Where are you? (nǎr = casual spoken, nǎlǐ = more formal)
Where do you live?
你住在哪里？
Nǐ zhù zài nǎlǐ?
Where do you live?

哪儿 (nǎr) and 哪里 (nǎlǐ) both mean "where." 哪儿 is common in northern China (Beijing), 哪里 is used everywhere. Either is correct.

03
Movement Verbs 动词
去、来、回: going places
Movement Verbs

去 · 来 · 回: go, come, return.

去
qù
to go (away from speaker)
来
lái
to come (toward speaker)
回
huí
to return / go back
I'm going to school
我去学校。
Wǒ qù xuéxiào.
I'm going to school.
Please come in
请进来！
Qǐng jìn lái!
Please come in!
I'm going home
我回家了。
Wǒ huí jiā le.
I'm going home / I went home.

去 vs 来: Think from the speaker's perspective. "I go to the store" (away from me) = 我去商店。 If someone is asking you to come to them: 你来这里 = "come here."

04
Location Words 方位词
Inside, on top, in front: describing where things are
方位词 Location Words

Inside. Above. In front.

上面
shàngmiàn
above / on top
前面
qiánmiàn
in front
里面
lǐmiàn
inside
后面
hòumiàn
behind
下面
xiàmiàn
below / under
The book is on the table
书在桌子上面。
Shū zài zhuōzi shàngmiàn.
The book is on top of the table.
The cat is under the chair
猫在椅子下面。
Māo zài yǐzi xiàmiàn.
The cat is under the chair.

Pattern: object + 在 + place + location word. You can also use shortened forms: 上 (shàng), 下 (xià), 里 (lǐ), 前 (qián), 后 (hòu).

Patterns

What are you doing right now?

Place a time word (今天, 现在) or location (在学校) before a verb to anchor it in time and place. Chinese relies on context, not verb tenses.

I'm studying right now
我现在在学习。
Wǒ xiànzài zài xuéxí.
I am studying right now. (在 before verb = ongoing action)
I eat at the restaurant
我在饭店吃饭。
Wǒ zài fàndiàn chī fàn.
I eat (a meal) at the restaurant.
What are you doing?
你在做什么？
Nǐ zài zuò shénme?
What are you doing?
I live in Beijing / I'm going home
我住在北京。我去图书馆学习。
Wǒ zhù zài Běijīng. Wǒ qù túshūguǎn xuéxí.
I live in Beijing. I'm going to the library to study.

在 + verb indicates an action happening right now (like English "-ing"). 我在吃饭 = "I am eating." Very common in conversation.

Dialogue 1 · Full

Where are you? What are you doing?

A
你现在在哪儿？
Nǐ xiànzài zài nǎr?
Where are you right now?
B
我在学校。我在学习。你呢？
Wǒ zài xuéxiào. Wǒ zài xuéxí. Nǐ ne?
I'm at school. I'm studying. And you?
A
我在家。我在看书。
Wǒ zài jiā. Wǒ zài kàn shū.
I'm at home. I'm reading a book.
B
你住在哪里？
Nǐ zhù zài nǎlǐ?
Where do you live?
A
我住在北京。学校在我家前面。
Wǒ zhù zài Běijīng. Xuéxiào zài wǒ jiā qiánmiàn.
I live in Beijing. The school is in front of my home.
B
太方便了！你今天去学校吗？
Tài fāngbiàn le! Nǐ jīntiān qù xuéxiào ma?
How convenient! Are you going to school today?
A
不去。今天我在家工作。明天去。
Bù qù. Jīntiān wǒ zài jiā gōngzuò. Míngtiān qù.
No. Today I'm working from home. I'll go tomorrow.

方便 (fāngbiàn) = convenient · 在家工作 = work from home · 在 + verb = ongoing action right now

Dialogue 2 · Full

Let's meet up.

A
你去饭店吗？
Nǐ qù fàndiàn ma?
Are you going to the restaurant?
B
去！饭店在哪儿？
Qù! Fàndiàn zài nǎr?
Yes! Where is the restaurant?
A
饭店在医院后面，商店前面。
Fàndiàn zài yīyuàn hòumiàn, shāngdiàn qiánmiàn.
The restaurant is behind the hospital, in front of the store.
B
好的！你现在在哪里？
Hǎo de! Nǐ xiànzài zài nǎlǐ?
Got it! Where are you right now?
A
我在火车站。我现在去饭店。
Wǒ zài huǒchēzhàn. Wǒ xiànzài qù fàndiàn.
I'm at the train station. I'm heading to the restaurant now.
B
太好了！我也去！我们一起去！
Tài hǎo le! Wǒ yě qù! Wǒmen yīqǐ qù!
Great! I'll go too! Let's go together!

好的 (hǎo de) = "got it / sure" · 一起 (yīqǐ) = together · Use location words to give directions: 医院后面 = "behind the hospital."
