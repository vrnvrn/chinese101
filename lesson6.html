01
会 & 能: Ability
Two ways to say "can"
Grammar: 会 vs 能

Two words for "can."

Both mean "can," but they're not interchangeable. 会 = learned ability. 能 = situational ability, capacity, or permission.

会
huì
Acquired skill: you learned to do it
"I can speak Chinese" (because I studied)
能
néng
Situational: physically able / allowed
"I can eat" (not sick today)
I can speak Chinese (learned skill)
我会说中文。
Wǒ huì shuō Zhōngwén.
I can speak Chinese.
Can you swim? (physical ability)
你会游泳吗？
Nǐ huì yóuyǒng ma?
Can you swim?
I can't come today (not available)
我今天不能来。
Wǒ jīntiān bù néng lái.
I can't come today.

Quick rule: 会 for skills (speak, drive, cook, swim). 能 for circumstances (can I enter? am I able to today?). In casual speech they often overlap: don't overthink it.

Transport 交通
Getting around with 坐
Transport Vocabulary

坐 + transport = "take the ___"

坐 (zuò) literally means "to sit." In Chinese you say "sit the bus" to mean "take the bus."

🚌
公共汽车
gōnggòng qìchē · bus
🚇
地铁
dìtiě · subway
🚕
出租车
chūzū chē · taxi
🚆
火车
huǒchē · train
✈️
飞机
fēijī · airplane
I take the subway to school
我坐地铁去学校。
Wǒ zuò dìtiě qù xuéxiào.
I take the subway to school.
How do you get to the airport?
你怎么去机场？
Nǐ zěnme qù jīchǎng?
How do you get to the airport?
I'm taking a plane to Beijing
我坐飞机去北京。
Wǒ zuò fēijī qù Běijīng.
I'm taking a plane to Beijing.

走路 (zǒu lù) = to walk · 骑车 (qí chē) = to ride a bike · 机场 (jīchǎng) = airport · 怎么 (zěnme) = how

04
了: Completion
Marking actions as done
Grammar: 了

了 signals that something happened.

了 (le) placed after a verb marks a completed action. It's not exactly a past tense: it indicates a change of state or a completed event. It can also signal a new situation.

I ate (action completed)
我吃了。
Wǒ chī le.
I ate. / I've eaten.
He went to school
他去学校了。
Tā qù xuéxiào le.
He went to school. / He has gone to school.
It started raining (new situation)
下雨了！
Xià yǔ le!
It's raining now! (it just started)
I've learned Chinese
我学了中文。
Wǒ xué le Zhōngwén.
I've studied Chinese.

了 does a lot of work in Chinese. For now, focus on: verb + 了 = the action is done. Also notice 太___了 from Lesson 5: the same 了 signals emotional intensity (a change of state in feeling).

Dialogue 1 · Full

A phone call.

A
喂！你好，请问你是李明吗？
Wèi! Nǐ hǎo, qǐngwèn nǐ shì Lǐ Míng ma?
Hello! Is this Li Ming?
B
是的，我是李明。你是谁？
Shì de, wǒ shì Lǐ Míng. Nǐ shì shuí?
Yes, I'm Li Ming. Who are you?
A
我是王芳。你现在在哪儿？
Wǒ shì Wáng Fāng. Nǐ xiànzài zài nǎr?
I'm Wang Fang. Where are you right now?
B
我在家。外面下雨了，我不能出去。
Wǒ zài jiā. Wàimiàn xià yǔ le, wǒ bù néng chūqù.
I'm at home. It's raining outside, I can't go out.
A
没关系！你会游泳吗？
Méi guānxi! Nǐ huì yóuyǒng ma?
No problem! Can you swim?
B
哈哈！会，我很喜欢游泳。
Hāhā! Huì, wǒ hěn xǐhuān yóuyǒng.
Haha! Yes, I really like swimming.
A
明天天气会好。我们去游泳，好吗？
Míngtiān tiānqì huì hǎo. Wǒmen qù yóuyǒng, hǎo ma?
Tomorrow the weather will be nice. Let's go swimming, okay?
B
好啊！说好了。再见！
Hǎo a! Shuō hǎo le. Zàijiàn!
Great! It's a plan. Goodbye!

外面 (wàimiàn) = outside · 出去 (chūqù) = to go out · 说好了 (shuō hǎo le) = it's agreed / it's a plan · 谁 (shuí) = who

Dialogue 2 · Full

Travel day.

A
你好！你去哪儿？
Nǐ hǎo! Nǐ qù nǎr?
Hi! Where are you going?
B
我去北京。我坐火车去。
Wǒ qù Běijīng. Wǒ zuò huǒchē qù.
I'm going to Beijing. I'm taking the train.
A
今天天气怎么样？
Jīntiān tiānqì zěnme yàng?
How's the weather today?
B
很好！晴天，不冷也不热。
Hěn hǎo! Qíngtiān, bù lěng yě bù rè.
Very nice! Sunny, not cold and not hot.
A
你以前去过北京吗？
Nǐ yǐqián qù guò Běijīng ma?
Have you been to Beijing before?
B
去过！我很喜欢北京。你呢？
Qù guò! Wǒ hěn xǐhuān Běijīng. Nǐ ne?
Yes, I have! I really like Beijing. And you?
A
我没去过，但我想去！祝你旅途愉快！
Wǒ méi qù guò, dàn wǒ xiǎng qù! Zhù nǐ lǚtú yúkuài!
I haven't been, but I want to go! Have a great trip!

不___也不___ (bù___ yě bù___) = neither ___ nor ___ · 以前 (yǐqián) = before / in the past · 过 (guò) = experiential particle · 祝 (zhù) = to wish
