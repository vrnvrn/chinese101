How to Tell Time
点 for the hour, 分 for the minutes, and the part of the day comes first
The Hour

Number plus 点.

To say a whole hour, put the number before 点 (diǎn), the word for "o'clock".

Number + 点
七点
qī diǎn
7:00
十二点
shí'èr diǎn
12:00

Two o'clock is the one to watch: say 两点 (liǎng diǎn), not 二点. 两 is the "two" you use before measure words, and 点 is one of them.

The Minutes

Number plus 分.

Minutes follow the hour, with 分 (fēn) for "minute". For minutes under ten, add 零 (líng, zero) first.

Hour + minutes + 分
七点十五分
qī diǎn shíwǔ fēn
7:15
Under ten, add 零
七点零五分
qī diǎn líng wǔ fēn
7:05

Minutes use the ordinary 二 (èr), never 两: 八点二十分 for 8:20, not 八点两十分.

Shortcuts

Half and quarter.

Three shortcuts you will hear constantly. Each one replaces the 分 count.

half past is 半
两点半
liǎng diǎn bàn
2:30
quarter past is 一刻
两点一刻
liǎng diǎn yí kè
2:15
three quarters is 三刻
两点三刻
liǎng diǎn sān kè
2:45

半 (bàn) is always safe. 一刻 and 三刻 are common in speech, but you can always fall back on 十五分 and 四十五分.

A Day on the Clock

My daily schedule.

One sentence for each part of the day. The order is subject, part of the day, time, then what you do.

我早上八点起床。
Wǒ zǎoshang bā diǎn qǐchuáng.
I get up at 8 in the morning.
我上午九点上班。
Wǒ shàngwǔ jiǔ diǎn shàngbān.
I go to work at 9 in the morning.
我中午十二点吃饭。
Wǒ zhōngwǔ shí'èr diǎn chī fàn.
I eat at 12 noon.
我下午五点上中文课。
Wǒ xiàwǔ wǔ diǎn shàng Zhōngwén kè.
I have Chinese class at 5 in the afternoon.
我晚上十点睡觉。
Wǒ wǎnshang shí diǎn shuìjiào.
I go to sleep at 10 at night.
