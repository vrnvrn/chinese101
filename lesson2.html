TENS (11–19)
十 + ones
十三 = 10 + 3 = 13
TWENTIES (20–29)
二十 + ones
二十七 = 20 + 7 = 27
ANY TENS
N + 十 + ones
三十五 = 3×10 + 5 = 35

The rule: tens digit + 十 + ones digit. The same logic scales to hundreds and thousands. Chinese arithmetic is transparent — the number tells you exactly how to say it.

Bigger Numbers

Ten, hundred, thousand.

Chinese number logic is very consistent. Once you know 1–10 and three multiplier words, you can build any number.

十
shí   10
Also the building block: 十一 = 11, 二十 = 20, 三十五 = 35
百
bǎi   100
一百 = 100, 两百 = 200, 三百五十 = 350
千
qiān   1,000
一千 = 1,000, 五千 = 5,000

Examples:  二十一 = 21  ·  三十八 = 38  ·  一百零五 = 105  ·  两千零八 = 2,008

两 liǎng is used instead of 二 when counting things (two of something): 两个人 = two people. More on that in Lesson 3 with measure words.

Age 年龄

How old are you?

Numbers immediately unlock one of the most common questions in Chinese. Just drop your age before 岁 (suì = years old).

How old are you? (casual)
你多大？
Nǐ duō dà?
How old are you? (lit. "You how big?")
How old are you? (more formal / for children)
你几岁？
Nǐ jǐ suì?
How old are you? (lit. "You how-many years-old?")
I am ___ years old
我___岁。
Wǒ ___ suì.
I am ___ years old.
我二十五岁。
Wǒ èrshíwǔ suì.
I am 25.
我三十岁。
Wǒ sānshí suì.
I am 30.
我十八岁。
Wǒ shíbā suì.
I am 18.

岁 suì always goes directly after the number — no "years" word needed separately. 我二十五岁 = I (am) 25 years-old.

02
Grammar
的, 喜欢, hobby & job patterns
Possessive Particle

的 (de) — the apostrophe-s of Chinese.

Place 的 between the owner and the thing owned. Think: owner + 的 + thing.

Pronoun + 的 + noun
我的爱好
wǒ de àihào
my hobby (lit. "I's hobby")
Pronoun + 的 + noun
你的工作
nǐ de gōngzuò
your job
Question using 的
你的爱好是什么？
Nǐ de àihào shì shénme?
What are your hobbies?

的 is neutral-tone and very light. It is often dropped in short, informal phrases: 我妈 (my mom), 我朋友 (my friend). But for learners, keeping it is safe.

Verb: 喜欢

我喜欢___ — I like ___.

喜欢 (xǐhuān) means "to like." It can take a noun or a verb phrase directly. No 很 needed — it is already a full verb.

Subject + 喜欢 + noun / verb phrase
我喜欢跑步。
Wǒ xǐhuān pǎobù.
I like running.
Subject + 喜欢 + noun
我喜欢哲学。
Wǒ xǐhuān zhéxué.
I like philosophy.
Negate with 不
我不喜欢唱歌。
Wǒ bù xǐhuān chànggē.
I don't like singing.
Question
你喜欢什么？
Nǐ xǐhuān shénme?
What do you like?

Substitution Drill

Swap in your own hobby.

The pattern stays the same. Only the hobby changes. Say each one aloud.

My hobby is ___
我的爱好是跑步。
Wǒ de àihào shì pǎobù.
My hobby is running.
Swap: reading
我的爱好是读书。
Wǒ de àihào shì dúshū.
My hobby is reading.
Swap: programming
我的爱好是编程。
Wǒ de àihào shì biānchéng.
My hobby is programming.
I like ___ and ___
我喜欢创业和哲学。
Wǒ xǐhuān chuàngyè hé zhéxué.
I like entrepreneurship and philosophy.

和 (hé) means "and" when connecting nouns. Use it to list multiple hobbies.

Question Word

为什么 / 因为 — Why / Because.

Now that you can say what you like, you can be asked why. 为什么 and 因为 always travel together.

为什么
wèishéme
why
因为
yīnwèi
because
Why do you like ___?
你为什么喜欢编程？
Nǐ wèishéme xǐhuān biānchéng?
Why do you like programming?
Because ___
因为编程很有意思。
Yīnwèi biānchéng hěn yǒu yìsi.
Because programming is very interesting.
Swap any hobby
因为跑步很好玩。
Yīnwèi pǎobù hěn hǎo wán.
Because running is a lot of fun.

有意思 yǒu yìsi = interesting  ·  好玩 hǎo wán = fun / enjoyable. Both work after 很 as adjective phrases.

Occupation Patterns

Ask and answer.

Most natural: What work do you do?
你做什么工作？
Nǐ zuò shénme gōngzuò?
What work do you do? (lit. "You do what work?")
Also common: What is your job?
你的工作是什么？
Nǐ de gōngzuò shì shénme?
What is your job?
I am a ___
我是程序员。/ 我是企业家。
Wǒ shì chéngyùyuán. / Wǒ shì qǐyèjiā.
I'm a programmer. / I'm an entrepreneur.
My job + hobby together
我是学生，我喜欢编程和人工智能。
Wǒ shì xuéshēng, wǒ xǐhuān biānchéng hé réngōng zhìnéng.
I'm a student. I like programming and AI.

A

B
我喜欢跑步和看电影。你呢？

I like running and watching movies. And you?
A
我的爱好是学中文和打篮球。
Wǒ de àihào shì xué Zhōngwén hé dǎ lánqiú.
My hobbies are studying Chinese and playing basketball.
B
太好了！你喜欢运动吗？
Tài hǎo le! Nǐ xǐhuān yùndòng ma?
That's great! Do you like exercise?
A
喜欢！我也喜欢瑜伽。
Xǐhuān! Wǒ yě xǐhuān yújiā.
Yes I do! I also like yoga.
B
你喜欢唱歌吗？
Nǐ xǐhuān chànggē ma?
Do you like singing?
A
不太喜欢。我更喜欢读书。
Bù tài xǐhuān. Wǒ gèng xǐhuān dúshū.
Not really. I prefer reading.

太好了 (tài hǎo le) = "great! / awesome!"  ·  不太 (bù tài) = "not really / not very"  ·  更 (gèng) = "even more / prefer"
