How to Study

Two modes. Both matter.

Every language skill is either production or consumption. Train both deliberately.

Production
  • 🗣 Speaking — output sounds, tones, words
  • ✍️ Writing — characters, pinyin, sentences
Consumption
  • 👂 Listening — tones, rhythm, real speech
  • 📖 Reading — characters, words, context

Most learners overconsume and underproduce. Flashcards and classes are consumption. Actually speaking and writing is production. You need both.

Your Goal

Pick a target. Work backwards.

A vague goal produces vague results. A specific target gives you a deadline, a location, and a study plan.

Example goal
I will pass the HSK 1 test in Johor on April 11th or May 16th.
Specific exam · specific city · two dates = a real plan.
Pick the exam
HSK 1, HSK 2, TOCFL, OPI — know exactly what you are being tested on.
Pick the date
Register in advance. A paid, booked seat is the best commitment device.
Work backwards
Count weeks to the exam. Assign vocabulary and skills to each week.

Pinyin 拼音

Your pronunciation map.

Pinyin is the standard system for writing Mandarin pronunciation using the Latin alphabet. It helps you say words before you can fully read characters. Every syllable has three parts.

4 tones + 1 neutral.

Pinyin Sounds

Watch out for these.

English instincts will occasionally try to sabotage the mission.

Retroflex: tongue curled slightly back.
zhōng, shì, chī
x / q / j
Fronted, lighter than English sh/ch/j.
xī, qǐng, jiào
c / z
c = "ts"  ·  z = "dz"
cài, zài

Say "ee" with rounded lips.
nǚ
r
Between English "r" and "zh".

02
Grammar
How Chinese builds sentences: and why it is simpler than you expect
Yes/No Questions

Statement + 吗 = question.

English flips word order to make a question. Mandarin adds 吗 at the end. That is it.

Statement
你是美国人。
Nǐ shì Měiguó rén.
You are American.
Question: add 吗
你是美国人吗？
Nǐ shì Měiguó rén ma?
Are you American?

Chinese does not rely on rising intonation alone for yes/no questions. Add 吗. Much cleaner than English inversion.

Core Sentence Patterns

Four patterns. All of Lesson 1.

Subject + Verb + Object
我叫 Maya。
Wǒ jiào Maya.
My name is Maya. (lit. "I am called Maya")
Subject + 是 + Noun
我是美国人。
Wǒ shì Měiguó rén.
I am American.
Subject + 很 + Adjective
我很好。
Wǒ hěn hǎo.
I am well. (很 acts as a linker, not just "very")
Statement + 吗 = Yes/No Question
你是学生吗？
Nǐ shì xuéshēng ma?
Are you a student?
Grammar Notes

What Chinese drops.

Mandarin is often simpler than English. Here is what it does not bother with:

No articles
No "a" or "the".
我是学生 = I am a student. Just like that.
Verbs don't conjugate
我是 · 你是 · 她是. The same 是 for everyone. No am/are/is changes.
Plurals are often context
Often no plural marking needed. Context carries it.
Tense by context
Verbs don't change for past/present/future. Time words or context handle it. For Day 1, stay present.

04
Radicals & Characters
How Chinese characters are built: and why it matters
Radicals 部首

Characters have structure.

Chinese characters are not random drawings. They are built from recurring components called radicals. Radicals often hint at meaning or category.

亻
Person
Appears in 你, 他, 们: all person-related characters
口
Mouth
In 吗, 呢, 叫, 吃: speech, sound, mouth
女
Woman
In 她, 妈: female-related characters

Good rule: radicals often give useful clues. Not: "the radical tells me the exact meaning." That is the sane version.

Spot the Radical

Find the clue inside.

你
nǐ: you
Left side: 亻 person radical. Right side hints at the sound.
她
tā: she
Left side: 女 woman radical. Tells you: female person.
吗
ma: question particle
Left side: 口 mouth radical. It is a spoken particle: makes sense.
国
guó: country
Enclosure structure: 囗 surrounds 玉. A territory enclosing something valuable.
